Questions:

What global issue does your project attempt to address?
Our app, Doc Digest, attempts to address language barriers associated with filling out health and legal documents. Many people who are not fluent in reading and writing in English struggle to fill out these documents, making it difficult for them to receive quality healthcare or completely understand both the health and legal documents they are required to fill out. With our app, Doc Digest, the users will be able to upload a document or picture and receive a translated document and a summary of the document they are filling out that is in simple terms and in their native language.

Why did your group choose this issue?
We chose this issue because not being a native English speaker can create a barrier when trying to understand and fill out important medical and legal documents. Since many documents are expected to be filled out in English, filling them out becomes difficult or even impossible for non-native speakers. Doc Digest makes sure that non-native speakers are still able to understand their health records and fill out legal documents, which they may have avoided or struggled with due to the language barrier and lack of accommodations for their needs.

How did researching this issue inform what your project would be?
Researching the topic we decided to create an app specifically focused on translating medical and legal documents because it is an area where a language barrier causes much greater struggle and stress for an individual. This information is crucial to understand so that non-fluent English speakers have agency in making legal and health choices.

What does your project do to address this issue?
Doc Digest allows users to receive translations and summaries of health and legal documents when they upload a PDF or photo, allowing them to more accurately understand and fill out the documents.

What does your project do differently from other preexisting solutions to this issue?
Doc Digest is specifically designed to address language barriers specifically regarding health and legal documents. This requires specific translations and summaries, not just general ones.

What evidence is there to prove that your project can be effective?
Translating documents in government institutions such as embassies can take a long time. Getting help translating and understanding these documents is often an expensive service, and time consuming. Doc Digest will allow the process of filling out legal and medical documents to be handled and understood fully by the individual themselves.

How did you build your project?
We built Doc Digest as an IOS app using XCode, an Apple IDE, Swift language, which creates the IOS app, and integrating Gemini 2.5 Flash.

*Why did you choose the particular tech stack that you did?
We used Gemini 2.5 Flash because it was the cheapest high-quality AI model we could find. We used Swift because we wanted to create a mobile app that could allow users to take pictures of documents. Finally, we chose to use XCode because it allowed us to see changes to our app in real time.

What challenges did you face?
Using XCode and learning Swift were challenges, as they were unfamiliar platforms to three of our team members. Also, since only one user in our group had a Mac computer, which is the only platform where XCode can be used and debugged, 3 out of 4 of us would have to use Mac computers provided to us in the Computer lab.

In the future, how would you face these challenges differently?
We would pick the tech stack further ahead of time and practice the language beforehand.

What did you learn?
We learned how to program in Swift and use XCode. We also gained some experience developing and designing the UI and handling AI API.

How can you further your learning from this project?
To further our learning, we can add a use case that allows the users to have the document read aloud to them and allows them to fill in the blanks using speech recognition and repeats the words for verification.

What’s next for your project?
Why is your project’s next step what it is? We want to be able to make DocDigest be able to accommodate to people with literacy issues in the future, making a fully audio controlled UI with simple buttons. We also want to add more language options and a fill-in-PDF feature in the future.
